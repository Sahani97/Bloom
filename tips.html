Plant Tips

Starting with houseplants can be intimidating, but not all plants require a green thumb. For beginners, there are plenty of hardy, low-maintenance options that thrive with minimal care. These plants tolerate irregular watering, less-than-ideal lighting, and the occasional forgotten routine.

Easy to Maintain Plants for Beginners

Fresh flowers can brighten any space, but some blooms fade faster than others. To keep your home vibrant and lively for longer, choose flowers with an extended lifespan. From the timeless beauty of Orchids to cheerful Gerbera Daisies, these flowers are known for their staying power.

Flowers That Last Long

Not all homes are blessed with bright, sunlit windows – but that doesn’t mean you can’t enjoy the beauty of indoor plants. Some plants thrive in low-light conditions, making them ideal for apartments, offices, or spaces with limited natural sunlight.

Plants for Apartments with Low Sunlight

For plant lovers with busy schedules (or the occasional forgetful streak), drought-tolerant plants are lifesavers. These low-water plants thrive in dry soil and can go weeks without watering. Cacti, succulents, and hardy plants like the Snake Plant store water in their leaves.
